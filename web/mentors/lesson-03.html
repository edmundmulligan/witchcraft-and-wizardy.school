Lesson 3 - Lists

Introduction

In this lesson, students are introduced to lists in HTML. Lists are used to organise information in a structured way, making it easier for users to read and understand. They help to create a more engaging and informative experience for users.

Some students may find definition lists and nested lists more challenging. Encourage them to experiment and practice with these types of lists to build their confidence and understanding.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lesson, students will have:

  • Understood the concept of lists in HTML.
  • Identified different types of lists and their purposes.
  • Created a simple web page using lists to structure the content.

Resources

Here are some resources that can help you as you guide your students through this lesson:

  • MDN Web Docs - HTML Lists
  • W3Schools HTML Lists

Challenges

Encourage students to experiment with different types of lists as they create their web page. Remind them that the goal is to have fun and learn, so they should feel free to be creative and make the page their own.

If they need inspiration for what to type in their lists, they can look at the famous witches and wizards in the facts page.
